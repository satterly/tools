@satterly Something's been nagging at me for a while in terms of how we could improve our Alerta setup. A lot of our monitoring feeds directly into Alerta, so it's our primary monitoring system. We have quite a few alerts that in isolation or in short duration aren't very serious (and would be classified as informational or warning). However if they're repeated (i.e. duplicate count increases) or not dealt with (i.e. their time since received increases), then they may be more serious. I'd love it if we could send an alert in with an escalation policy and if certain conditions were met when the alert repeats, Alerta would escalate them by increasing the severity.

An example, a security upgrade is available to apply. Let's say we work on a 24 hour patching schedule for security upgrades. If an upgrade has been available for less than 24 hours, it's a warning, but if takes longer than that it should be upgraded to a security alert.

We've got a lot of alerts that we could raise at an initially lower level, reducing alert fatigue. Some of these alerts may even be auto-resolved by our automation or manually in bulk before there's a chance for them to escalate, but we want some visibility from the beginning. Alerta works very well at giving us an idea of anything vaguely important happening across our infrastructure.

We could maybe build this into our monitoring scripts, but at the moment they're very simple because they just look at the current system state and fire an alert without considering what the current state on Alerta might be. I'm thinking putting this into Alerta as a plugin might be the way to go, but wondered what your thoughts were on the best way to handle it or even whether you think it's a terrible idea and there's a better way of handling it.
