Nick Satterly
@satterly
Jan 13 08:39 UTC
"indeterminate" is the default previous severity. ie. a alert progresses through several different statuses and the next status is influenced by the severity of a new alert and its current status. New alerts don't have a previous status so they are given the status "indeterminate" which basically means "not defined". it is referenced from this RFC (without definition) ... https://tools.ietf.org/html/rfc5674
@danfoxley ^
@roboticpuppies Using oembed is one way to do what you want if you need total flexibility and don't mind coding. If you just want predefined filters then I suggest using "alert status indicators". See alerta/alerta-webui#203
